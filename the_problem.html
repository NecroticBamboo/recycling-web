Rate

Nowadays people tend to buy all kinds of things. They buy them, use them for some time and then throw them away. This causes a lot of problems not only to the people but also to the surrounding environment. On the graph below you can see a rate of household waste from non-household recycling in London. It has jumped from 40 metric tons in 2000 to almost 104 metric tons in 2019. It means that we waste more than we can recycle.
